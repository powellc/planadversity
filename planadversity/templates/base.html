Hack Labs

A place for middle school and high school students

About Hack Labs

More and more our world is being run by devices. Little time passes in our lives when we are not connected. Young people in this world are often referred to as "digital natives" but aside from comfort using these devices, that means very little

Our goal with Hack Labs is to provide a unique environment where students can imagine what they want to build, and then find support from engaged mentors to turn those ideas into concrete learning experiences.

How Hack Labs Work

Hack Labs operate on a monthly cycle and,
work is done one day per week.

Week One

Exploration

The first week is open to all ages. Kids are invited to come for all or some of the session and get to explore a number of preset stations that cover a topic in technology, whether software or hardware or both.

Weeks Two & Three

Lab Work

The next two weeks are working days. These days the hack lab is limited to only those students who have proposals that have been accepted by a mentor.

Weeks Four

Demo Day

Finally, the cycle concludes with a demo day. Participants are asked to either give a really quick presentation, or present a video of what worked, what didn't and what they learned about their chosen goal.
